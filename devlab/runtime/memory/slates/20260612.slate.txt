## DESIGN COHERENCE GAP — Granny's Real Role (2026-06-12 15:10)

**Current State: Incoherent**
- Granny exists as a daemon but isn't the actual queue manager
- Builders (CC, Igor, DS) still pull tickets from queue directly
- No handshake protocol (builder doesn't acknowledge receipt to Granny)
- No offer expiry (ticket stays assigned even if builder never picks it up)
- No cooldown (failed builder gets re-offered immediately)
- No evidence that Granny can actually launch/wake builders

**Intended Design (per Akien):**
1. **Granny is the single queue arbiter** — only she decides who gets what ticket
2. **Granny offers a ticket to a builder** — posts to their mailbox
3. **Builder's shim acknowledges receipt** — replies to Granny (within 2 min timeout)
4. **If no ack within 2 min** — offer expires, ticket returns to queue, builder on 10-min cooldown
5. **Different builder CAN get it immediately** — only the failed builder is cooled down
6. **Builder executes ticket, updates status to done** — Granny observes completion
7. **Granny can launch/wake builders** — not just observe them
8. **Granny may need inference** — to decide which builder gets which ticket type

**Why This Matters:**
- CC burns tokens and stops working → Granny wakes CC or routes to DS
- DS fails on inference → Granny routes to CC or escalates
- Single authoritative queue manager prevents double-processing and race conditions

**Current Blockers to Implementation:**
- Builders don't have a "receive offer from Granny, reply with ack" RPC mechanism
- No cooldown state tracking in queue
- Granny can't launch builders (only observe them)
- No inference-based routing logic in Granny

**Decision Required:**
Implement the handshake protocol fully, or revert to builders managing their own queue consumption?
