## Decision narrative

Adopted from +ddai's design-reviewer: structural anti-patterns are directives ("this violates the abstraction boundary — must change"); behavioral complexity is questions only ("why is this logic complex?"). UU's audits currently treat everything as a directive. The distinction is accurate: an AI can safely assert structural violations, but behavioral depth often has a non-obvious reason the AI cannot see. Applying this to audit-design and audit-ticket reduces false-positive directives and makes audits more trustworthy.

## Hypothesis
After T-audit-domain-humility ships, audit outputs distinguish STRUCTURAL (directive) from BEHAVIORAL (question) findings — audits are more accurate and fewer behavioral findings are incorrectly flagged as must-change.

## Measurement Signal
Next audit-design run produces findings in two categories. Behavioral findings are phrased as questions ("why...?") not directives ("must change...").
