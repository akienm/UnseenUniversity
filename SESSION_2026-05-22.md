### 2. Wrote QE in the Age of AI

`lab/docs/QE_in_the_Age_of_AI.md`

A strategic brief for Chad (Quality Engineering COE) to take to directors. Argues that AI acceleration makes QE more important on two axes:

1. **AI as force multiplier on existing QE expertise** — proven patterns get leverage; blank canvas gets variance
2. **QE as architect of the compilation loop** — someone has to decide what gets compiled, validate it's correct, and measure whether it improved things. That's a QE skill set at a systems level.

The spine: AI moved the bottleneck from creation to correctness and ownership. Chad's team is the answer to the new bottleneck.

### 3. Wrote Compiled Inference

`lab/docs/Compiled_Inference.md`

A full article draft (~1,100 words) for Chad first, directors second. Technical enough for Chad to cut before handing upward. Four sections:

1. **The creation/ownership gap** — AI rates are rising; organizations are paying inference prices for things that don't need inference
2. **Compiled inference** — freeform (1000 tokens) → skill (300 tokens) → script (100 tokens); each layer removes ambiguity and reduces cost of ownership
3. **Feedback loops** — how the system improves itself over time: hypothesis → measure → outcome → refine; structured loops compound, informal learning doesn't
4. **Tooling and Dos and Don'ts** — including a new section on building QE processes in: formal design, design audits, ticket audits, pre-build audits, post-build audits, hypothesis verification. AI doesn't gripe about process. It just does it, right the first time, if you demand it.
